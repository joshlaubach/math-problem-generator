LATEX TO SPOKEN ENGLISH CONVERSION RULES
=========================================

Apply ALL of these rules when writing narration text.
Narration must be plain English — zero LaTeX syntax allowed.

BASIC OPERATIONS
────────────────
x + y           → "x plus y"
x - y           → "x minus y"
x * y           → "x times y"  or  "x multiplied by y"
x / y           → "x divided by y"
x = y           → "x equals y"
x ≠ y           → "x is not equal to y"
x < y           → "x is less than y"
x > y           → "x is greater than y"
x ≤ y           → "x is less than or equal to y"
x ≥ y           → "x is greater than or equal to y"
x ≈ y           → "x is approximately y"

EXPONENTS AND ROOTS
────────────────────
x^2             → "x squared"
x^3             → "x cubed"
x^n             → "x to the nth power"  or  "x to the power of n"
x^{-1}          → "x to the negative one"  or  "the reciprocal of x"
x^{1/2}         → "the square root of x"
\sqrt{x}        → "the square root of x"
\sqrt[3]{x}     → "the cube root of x"
\sqrt[n]{x}     → "the nth root of x"
e^x             → "e to the x"
e^{-x}          → "e to the negative x"

FRACTIONS
─────────
\frac{a}{b}     → "a over b"
\frac{1}{2}     → "one half"
\frac{1}{3}     → "one third"
\frac{2}{3}     → "two thirds"
\frac{x+1}{2}   → "the quantity x plus 1, over 2"
-\frac{b}{2a}   → "negative b over 2a"

ALGEBRA
───────
(x-1)(x+2)      → "the quantity x minus 1, times the quantity x plus 2"
|x|             → "the absolute value of x"
±               → "plus or minus"
∓               → "minus or plus"
\pm             → "plus or minus"

TRIGONOMETRY
────────────
\sin x          → "sine of x"   or  "sin x"
\cos x          → "cosine of x" or  "cos x"
\tan x          → "tangent of x"
\csc x          → "cosecant of x"
\sec x          → "secant of x"
\cot x          → "cotangent of x"
\sin^2 x        → "sine squared of x"
\sin^{-1} x     → "arcsine of x"  or  "the inverse sine of x"
\cos^{-1} x     → "arccosine of x"
\tan^{-1} x     → "arctangent of x"
\theta          → "theta"
\alpha          → "alpha"
\beta           → "beta"
\phi            → "phi"

LOGARITHMS AND EXPONENTIALS
────────────────────────────
\log x          → "log of x"
\log_2 x        → "log base 2 of x"
\ln x           → "the natural log of x"
\log_{10} x     → "log base 10 of x"
e               → "e"  (do not say "Euler's number" unless introducing it)

CALCULUS
────────
\frac{dy}{dx}       → "dy dx"  or  "the derivative of y with respect to x"
f'(x)               → "f prime of x"
f''(x)              → "f double prime of x"
\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}   → "the second derivative of y with respect to x"
\int f(x)\,dx       → "the integral of f of x, dx"
\int_a^b f(x)\,dx   → "the integral from a to b of f of x, dx"
\lim_{x \to 0}      → "the limit as x approaches zero"
\lim_{x \to \infty} → "the limit as x approaches infinity"
\sum_{i=1}^{n}      → "the sum from i equals 1 to n"
\prod               → "the product"
\infty              → "infinity"
\partial            → "the partial derivative"

LINEAR ALGEBRA
──────────────
\vec{v}         → "the vector v"
\mathbf{A}      → "matrix A"
A^{-1}          → "A inverse"
A^T             → "A transpose"
\det(A)         → "the determinant of A"
\|v\|           → "the magnitude of v"  or  "the norm of v"
\cdot           → "dot"  (for dot product)
\times          → "cross"  (for cross product)

SET THEORY
──────────
\in             → "is an element of"
\notin          → "is not an element of"
\subset         → "is a subset of"
\cup            → "union"
\cap            → "intersection"
\emptyset       → "the empty set"
\mathbb{R}      → "the real numbers"
\mathbb{Z}      → "the integers"
\mathbb{N}      → "the natural numbers"

GREEK LETTERS
─────────────
\pi             → "pi"
\Delta          → "delta"  (change in)
\Sigma          → "sigma"  (summation)
\lambda         → "lambda"
\mu             → "mu"
\sigma          → "sigma"
\epsilon        → "epsilon"
\delta          → "delta"
\omega          → "omega"

SPECIAL FORMS
─────────────
x \to \infty    → "x approaches infinity"
f: A \to B      → "f maps A to B"
\forall x       → "for all x"
\exists x       → "there exists an x"
\Rightarrow     → "implies"
\Leftrightarrow → "if and only if"
